## The Turning Point

  The energy sector reached an inflection point in 2026. Battery storage costs, which had been declining steadily for a decade, reached a threshold where storage became cheaper than natural gas peaking plants in most markets.

  <KeyInsight>
    Battery storage costs fell below $100/kWh for the first time, making renewable energy with storage economically viable without subsidies in 80% of major markets.
  </KeyInsight>

  ## Market-Wide Implications

  This shift has cascading effects across the entire energy ecosystem:

  - **Grid Stability**: Renewable energy is no longer intermittent; storage solves the temporal mismatch
  - **Utility Model**: Baseload coal and gas plants become stranded assets
  - **Investment Flows**: Capital is rapidly redirecting from fossil fuels to renewable infrastructure
  - **Emissions Trajectory**: We now have a feasible path to decarbonization

  ## What Changed?

  <ChartNarrative title="Battery Cost Decline" description="20-year trajectory showing the exponential decline in lithium-ion battery costs">
    [Chart: Battery costs declining from $1,200/kWh in 2010 to $98/kWh in 2026]

  Manufacturing scale-up in Asia, combined with:

  1. **Raw Material Optimization**: Better extraction and recycling of lithium and cobalt
  2. **Cell Chemistry**: New cathode formulations increased energy density
  3. **Supply Chain Integration**: Vertical integration reduced costs by 15-20%
  4. **Scale Economics**: Production volumes exceeded 500 GWh annually

  ## Looking Ahead

  The energy transition is no longer aspirational—it's inevitable. The only question now is how quickly utilities and governments adapt their infrastructure to this new reality.
